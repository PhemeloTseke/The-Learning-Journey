Call me Mel

My name is Phemelo Tseke, aka Mel. My journey with Front-end development was an unconventional one. Before, the jump into web development, my focus was set on UI/UX Design, but I was quite unfulfilled with the idea of just creating high fidelity prototypes. I needed the thrill of putting together the final product. Until this particular moment, all my learning related to Front-end development has been theoretical. Enrolling in The Zuri Internship Program I am excited to practice my skills, learn from the wonderful Zuri team and grow.

When I'm not binge watching The Amazing World of Gumball I'm reading and practicing efficiency within my programming. I believe in the tech industry you are always learning as things evolve, and being a new-comer it helps familiarizing myself with both previous teachings and newer ones.

Self-Taught? How?

I am self-taught front-end developer who, before writing any code, watched a lot of tutorials to grasp concepts and learn how the languages I was learning are you used to programme. I started with learning HTML with Programming with Mosh. Two month buried in theory I believe it's time to put my knowledge and skill to test. HTML was very enjoyable and really sets a stepping stone to being excited about programming in general.

I later moved to CSS with Traversy Media and fell in love immediately with it. Vanilla CSS is such a powerful tool which fascinates me greatly, which in turn makes me quite excited to learning CSS frameworks and libraries in the future. I am a very artistic person (hence I initially gravitated towards design) so I immersed myself into CSS theory and I'm looking forward to learning advanced features.
